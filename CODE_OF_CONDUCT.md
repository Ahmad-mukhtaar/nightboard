# Code of Conduct

## Our Standard

Nightboard is a small public project. The expectation is straightforward: be respectful, be specific, and keep collaboration useful.

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- giving constructive, technical feedback
- assuming good intent while still being clear about problems
- respecting different levels of experience
- focusing criticism on the work, not the person
- being patient when discussing bugs, regressions, or design disagreements

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- harassment, insults, or personal attacks
- discriminatory or demeaning language
- trolling, hostility, or intentionally derailing discussions
- publishing private information without permission
- repeatedly ignoring project boundaries after being asked to stop

## Scope

This applies within project spaces, including issues, pull requests, and discussions tied to Nightboard.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may remove, edit, or reject comments, issues, commits, code, or other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct.

## Reporting

For conduct issues, open a private report through GitHub or contact the maintainer through the repository owner account.
